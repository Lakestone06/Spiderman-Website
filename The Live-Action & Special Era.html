🎬 The Live-Action & Special Era

🏙️ The Amazing Spider-Man (1977)

Marking the very first time Spider-Man was portrayed by a live-action actor, this late-1970s CBS series brought the wall-crawler into the real world. Though it lacked the colorful supervillains of the comic books—forcing Peter to fight corporate criminals and mind-controlling scientists instead—it was a massive ratings hit and pioneered early live-action stunt rigging

👤 Actor: Nicholas Hammond

📅 Era: 1977 - 1979

🎥 Show: The Amazing Spider-Man (Live-Action)

🎬 Key Director: Cliff Bole

🤖 Supaidāman - Toei's Spider-Man (1978)

In this legendary Japanese live-action adaptation produced by Toei, motorcycle racer Takuya Yamashiro receives spider-powers from an alien warrior from Planet Spider. This iconic show radically reinvented the character, giving Spider-Man a giant mech named Leopardon to fight giant monsters—a concept so successful it permanently inspired the Super Sentai (Power Rangers) genre.

👤 Actor: Shinji Tōdō

📅 Era: 1978 - 1979

🎥 Show: Spider-Man (Japanese Live-Action)

🎬 Key Director: Kōichi Takemoto

🕵️‍♂️ Spider-Noir (2026)

Stepping into a moody, stylized alternate universe, this live-action series follows an aging, down-on-his-luck private investigator named Ben Reilly in 1930s New York City. Forced to confront his past as the city's sole, gritty protector, the series leans heavily into a dark, black-and-white neo-noir aesthetic packed with mobsters and monsters.

👤 Actor: Nicolas Cage

📅 Era: 2026 - Present

🎥 Show: Spider-Noir

🎬 Showrunner: Oren Uziel / Steve Lightfoot

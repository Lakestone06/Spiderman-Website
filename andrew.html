🕸️ The Amazing Spider-Man (2012)

Released in 2012, Marc Webb's The Amazing Spider-Man rebooted the franchise with a darker, more modern tone and introduced Andrew Garfield as a witty, skateboard-riding Peter Parker. The film focuses on a more mystery-driven origin story, following Peter as he uncovers secrets about his long-lost parents while falling for his first high school love, Gwen Stacy (Emma Stone). When his father's former research partner transforms into the monstrous Lizard, Peter must master his new web-slinging abilities to protect New York City from a biological catastrophe.

🕸️ The Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014)

Released in 2014, The Amazing Spider-Man 2 increased the scale of the action while deeply exploring the central romance between Peter and Gwen. The story finds Peter fully embracing his superhero identity but facing a complex web of threats, including the glowing, electricity-manipulating Electro (Jamie Foxx) and his childhood friend Harry Osborn (Dane DeHaan). Everything builds toward a heavily emotional and tragic finale that permanently altered Peter’s life and left a lasting impact on comic book cinema history.

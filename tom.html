🕷️ Tom Holland Universe

🕸️Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017)

Released in 2017, Jon Watts’ Spider-Man: Homecoming integrated the web-slinger fully into the Marvel Cinematic Universe with a fresh, lighthearted high school comedy vibe. Starring Tom Holland as a younger Peter Parker, the film follows the eager teenager as he tries to balance algebra homework with proving himself to his mentor, Tony Stark (Iron Man). Peter’s desire to join the Avengers hits a dangerous roadblock when he discovers that the father of his high school crush is actually the Vulture (Michael Keaton), a menacing black-market arms dealer utilizing alien technology.

🕸️Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019)

Released in 2019, Spider-Man: Far From Home serves as a heavy emotional follow-up to Avengers: Endgame, dealing with Peter’s grief after losing his mentor. Looking for a break from hero duties, Peter goes on a school trip to Europe, but Nick Fury hijacks his vacation to deal with mysterious elemental monsters destroying cities across the continent. Peter teams up with a new hero from another dimension named Mysterio (Jake Gyllenhaal), leading to an illusion-filled battle of wits that concludes with a massive cliffhanger exposing Spider-Man's secret identity to the entire world.

🕸️Spider-Man: No Way Home (2021)

Released in 2021, Spider-Man: No Way Home became a massive global cinematic event by fully breaking open the multiverse. Desperate to restore his anonymity, Peter asks Doctor Strange to cast a spell making everyone forget his identity, but the spell goes terribly wrong and tears open the fabric of reality. This mistake drags iconic villains from past universes—and eventually Tobey Maguire and Andrew Garfield’s Spider-Men—into his world, leading to a legendary, highly emotional team-up to save their lives
